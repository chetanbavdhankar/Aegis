Spain's meteorological agency sounded the alarm at dawn — maximum red alert, extreme danger. For twelve and a half hours, that warning moved through institutional channels while floodwater rose through Valencia's streets.

The gap wasn't an accident. It was a system failure — three failures, stacked on top of each other.

The Communication Gap.
Agencies alert. Governments deliberate. Citizens wait. The chain between a warning and the person who needs it is broken by design.
02
Language.
When the person who needs help doesn't speak the language of the person who can give it — the gap becomes a wall. Every warning. Every time.
03
No Single Picture.
Responders can't see individual cries for help and the full crisis at once. One person or the crowd — never both. Triage becomes guesswork.
